發刊詞

勇於求索，詩意人生— 對真理的追求高於生命

張一方(Yi-Fang Chang)

“欲變節而從俗兮，媿易初而屈志”（屈原：《九章.思美人》），“亦余心之所善兮，雖九死其猶未悔”（屈原：《離騷》）。超心理學作為當代世界科學發展的前沿之一，具有眾多撲朔迷離的神奇現象，令人困惑，令人癡迷，我們相信其中一定蘊含著值得我們不懈追尋的真理。從伏羲開始，中華傳統文化起碼已經6500年以上，包括儒釋道、易學和風水等，博大精深，豐富多彩。其中超心理學現象，特別是道教、密宗和某些少數民族的獨特文化，內涵深遠，綿延不絕。培根說過: “探求真理——向它求愛，認識真理——與它同在，信仰真理——為它而樂，畢竟是人性中最崇高的美德。” 愛因斯坦指出 : “對真理和知識的追求併為之奮鬥，是人的最高品質之一”。

“路漫漫其修遠兮，吾將上下而求索”（《離騷》）。 “閑愁最苦。 休去倚危欄，斜陽正在，煙柳斷腸處” （辛棄疾：《摸魚兒》）。中華傳統文化博大精深，世界超心理學研究神秘莫測。 人生難得，每一個不甘虛度時光的人。 這些都值得我們深入探索。 2011年10月23-24日和2016年11月19-21日在昆明兩次召開“中華傳統文化與超心理學”學術研討會都取得重大成功，並且會後都出版了會議文集，這是在中國大陸正式出版的超心理學方面的學術著作。我們的觀點是：對科學和真理的探索無禁區，對真善美的追求永無止境。迄今超心理學研究有不足，甚至有錯誤，有陷阱，但是只要敢於認錯，我們將繼續一如既往地奮勇向前。何況是對那些雲裡霧裡未知的探索，對那些神秘而且可能千變萬化的靈異現象。

“莫聽穿林打葉聲，何妨吟嘯且徐行。 竹杖芒鞋輕勝馬，誰怕？ 一蓑煙雨任平生。 … 回首向來蕭瑟處，歸去，也無風雨也無晴“（蘇軾：《定風波》）。不畏風雨，怡然自得。 儘管始終存在某些渾水摸魚，欺世盜名，追名逐利之徒，似乎天機不可洩露，許多人為此已經犧牲。 “寧可枝條抱香死，何曾吹落北風中”（鄭思肖：《寒菊》）。但夸父逐日的本性仍吸引無數追求真理的人不斷地赴湯蹈火，前仆後繼。在此過程中，按照中華傳統文化，探索者必須認真修德，正念正行，方能不斷精進，否則將可能誤人害己。

雖然國內外對超心理學的瞭解由來已久，但國際超心理學正式的學術研究開始於英國1882年2月20日成立的超心理學學會，首任理事長是劍橋大學教授E. Sidgwick，至今已經144年。儘管其也有起伏，但他們的堅持，令人欽佩。迄今已經有遍及歐美亞澳非數十個國家，包括越南都開展了超心理學研究。面對世界我們絕不能故步自封，閉門造車，必須與國際學術界展開交流，以便取長補短，才能發現和發揮我們的特長，“滿園春色關不住，一枝紅杏出牆來”，讓全世界知道我們的求索。時光如箭，在小小的“地球村”如果我們裹足不前，世界科學也仍然進步。“兩岸猿聲啼不住，輕舟已過萬重山”，當年令人驚訝不已的耳朵認字、非眼視覺，現代神經生物學已經基本解決了這些疑難，但身-心-靈的三維甚至多維世界等一系列神秘現象仍然展現出無窮的魅力。

熱愛-求索-堅持-弘揚將是我們研究超心理學的四部曲。不畏寒暑，遑顧春秋，每日汲汲於求索，終生營營乎真諦。 白駒過隙，樂在其中，詩意人生，豈不快哉！

我們的宗旨將形象地體現在會標上，一個中國傳統文化的太極圖加上一個國際上表示超心理學的符號Psi，而且用表示生命的綠色修飾。這表明我們既立足於中華傳統文化，又結合世界的發展，俯視古今六千年，縱觀全球數萬里。有神奇意蘊的是太極圖也是量子力學公認的領袖Bohr表示互補原理的族徽，Psi又是量子理論中的波函數。當然我們也絕不排斥形形色色、紛繁複雜的各種超心理學現象。中華超心理學必須建立在世界與自身的雙重認識上，才能有光明和無限廣闊的未來。中華傳統文化具有悠久而豐富的超心理學底蘊，完全能夠在世界超心理學研究中獨放異彩，做出我們華人力所能及的貢獻。

回首往事，一代代薪火相傳，我們彷彿又聽到無數兒童和大眾面對各種奇異現象，不約而同發出的驚呼聲。展望未來，我們彷彿看到偉大祖國的花園裡百花齊放，中國優秀的傳統文化一定會推向全球，並融入世界的大潮。即使我們個人悠然西去，但仍然可以聽到後繼者不斷前行的堅定腳步聲，我們求索的夢也仍然將縈繞在這一輝煌的聖境中。

Proposal of the International Journal of Parapsychology and Traditional Chinese Culture

A life of exploration and poetry (never hesitate to study in a life of poetry) — the pursuit of truth is above life itself.

Albert So translator

“I wish to change my ways and follow the customs, but I am ashamed to have changed my initial intentions and compromised my principles.” (Qu Yuan, “Nine Chapters: Thinking of the Beautiful One”). “What my heart desires, though I die nine times, I will not regret.” (Qu Yuan, “Li Sao”). Parapsychology, as a forefront of the contemporary science, possesses numerous confusing, bewildering and mysterious phenomena, which are both perplexing and fascinating. We believe that it must contain truths worthy of our relentless pursuit. From Fuxi onwards, traditional Chinese culture has been existing for at least 6500 years, including but not limited to different schools of thoughts such as Confucianism, Buddhism, Taoism, I Ching, and Feng Shui, etc., profound and diverse. Among them, parapsychological phenomena, especially those related to Taoism, Tantric Buddhism, and the unique cultures of certain ethnic minorities, have profound connotations, continually uninterrupted. Bacon said, “The pursuit of truth — to love it, to know it — to be with it, to believe in it — to rejoice in it, is, after all, the noblest virtue of human nature.” Einstein pointed out, “The searching and striving for truth and knowledge, and the struggling for it, is one of the highest virtues of the mankind.”

“The road ahead is long and arduous, I will continue to search, up and down.” (Li Sao). “Idle sorrow is the most bitter. Do not lean against the perilous railing, for the setting sun shines brightly, and the willows and smoke evoke a heartbreaking scene” (Xin Qiji, “Touching Fish”). Chinese traditional culture is profound and extensive while parapsychology research is mysterious and unpredictable. Life is precious, and everyone who does not want to waste time should cherish it. These two areas, which are tightly interwinding with each other, are all worthy of our in-depth exploration. The two academic conferences on “Chinese Traditional Culture and Parapsychology” held in Kunming on October 23-24, 2011, and November 19-21, 2016, were both highly successful, and the conference proceedings were published afterwards. These two publications could be the pioneering academic works on parapsychology in mainland China. Our view is that there is no forbidden zone in the exploration of science and truth, and the pursuit of truth, goodness, and beauty is endless. To date, parapsychology research has shortcomings, even errors and pitfalls, but as long as we dare to admit our mistakes, we will continue to forge ahead as always. Especially when it comes to exploring those unknown and shrouded in mist, and to unveiling those mysterious and ever-changing paranormal phenomena.

“Do not just listen to the sound of rain pattering through the trees, why not chant and stroll leisurely? A bamboo staff and straw sandals are lighter than a horse, who's afraid? A raincoat made of straw and misty rain are all I need for a lifetime… Looking back at the desolate place I came from, and returning, there is neither wind nor rain, neither sunshine nor gloom.” (Su Shi, “Ding Feng Bo”) When we do something great, we should not be afraid of wind and rain, i.e. obstacles and difficulties, but be content and at ease. Although there are always some opportunists, charlatans, and profit-seekers, claiming that the secrets of heaven cannot be revealed. But many previous researchers have already sacrificed themselves for it. “Rather die embracing fragrance on the branch than be blown away by the north wind.” (Zheng Sixiao, “Winter Chrysanthemum”) It is encouraging to know the nature of Kuafu chasing the sun still attracts countless truth-seekers to go through difficulties, one after another. In this process, according to traditional Chinese culture, keen explorers must earnestly cultivate virtue, maintain righteous thoughts and actions, in order to continuously improve; otherwise, they may mislead others and harm themselves.

Although the understanding of parapsychology has had a long history both domestically and internationally, formal international academic research in parapsychology only began with the establishment of the Society for Parapsychology in the U.K. on February 20, 1882, with Professor E. Sidgwick of the Cambridge University as its first president, thus marking 144 years of history. Despite its ups and downs, their perseverance is admirable. To date, parapsychological research has spread to dozens of countries across Europe, America, Asia, Australia, and Africa, including Vietnam. Facing the world, we must not be complacent or work in isolation; we must engage in exchanges with the international academic community to learn from each other's strengths and weaknesses, discovering and developing our own strengths. “Spring's beauty cannot be confined within the garden wall; a branch of red apricot blossoms peeks over the wall.” (Poem from the Sung Dynasty) We must work hand in hand with the world regarding our pursuit. Time flies, and even if we remain stagnant in this small “global village”, world science will still progress. “The cries of monkeys on both banks could not stop the light boat from passing through ten thousand mountains.” (Poem from the Tang Dynasty) The once astonishing phenomena of reading with the ears and non-eye vision have studied by modern neurobiology, while a long list of mysterious phenomena, such as the three-dimensional and even multi-dimensional world of body, mind, and spirit, still exhibits boundless charm.

Passion, Exploration, Perseverance, and Communication (Promotion) are the four-part approaches to our research mission in parapsychology. Undeterred by heat or cold, regardless of the changing seasons, we shall dedicate ourselves daily to the pursuit of truth, striving throughout our lives. Time flies, but we find joy in the process with a poetic life. What could be more delightful!

Our mission is vividly reflected in our logo which features a traditional Chinese yin-yang Tai Chi symbol underpinned by the internationally recognized symbol, ψ, pronounced as Psi, for parapsychology, which is depicted in green to represent life. This demonstrates that we are grounded in traditional Chinese culture while also considering global development, taking a broad view of six thousand years of history and spanning thousands of miles around the world. The Tai Chi symbol is also the emblem of Bohr, the recognized leader in quantum mechanics, representing the complementarity principle. And Psi is also the wave function in one fundamental equation in quantum physics, the famous Schrodinger equation. Of course, we do not exclude the diverse and complex phenomena of parapsychology. Chinese parapsychology must be built upon a dual understanding of the world and ourselves to guarantee a bright and infinitely broad future. Traditional Chinese culture possesses a long and rich foundation in parapsychology, fully capable of shining brightly in global parapsychological research and demonstrating that we Chinese can contribute to the best of our ability.

Looking back, as the torch is passed down from generation to generation, we seem to hear again the spontaneous exclamations of countless children and the general public as they witness various wondrous phenomena. Looking at the future, we seem to see flowers of different species blooming in the garden of our great motherland, and China's excellent traditional culture will surely be promoted globally and integrated into the world's tide. Even when we ourselves pass away peacefully, we can still hear the firm footsteps of our successors moving forward, and our dreams of exploration will still linger in this piece of magnificent sacred land.
